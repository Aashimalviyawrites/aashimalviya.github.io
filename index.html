About

I write because I cannot live quietly with my thoughts. They insist on becoming lines, and the lines insist on becoming stories.

I’m a student at PM SHRI Kendriya Vidyalaya, where my words first learned how to leave my notebook and find their way into the world. Twice, my writing appeared in the Reader’s Corner of The Free Press Journal, where my words first stepped beyond my notebook and into print.

I move between shayari and prose, between Hindi and English, between memory and imagination.

I believe in small moments—the pause before a reply, the silence after a song, the look someone gives when they don’t have the right words.

If you find something here that feels like your own thought, then my work has done what it was meant to do.

— Aashi Malviya
