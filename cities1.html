Growing up across cities

March 2026

When people talk about their “childhood best friend,” I usually just smile.

My childhood was different.

It was scattered across cities because my parents’ work required frequent transfers. Changing schools, leaving friends behind, and moving into new houses were never unusual for me.

When I look back now, four cities shaped who I am today: Indore, Jodhpur, Mumbai, and Shajapur.

I was born in Indore. My family consists of six members — my grandparents, parents, my younger sister, and me. Since my earliest days, I have shared a strong bond with each of them. It is a unique kind of attachment that is difficult to explain, but easy to feel.

At home, I was the “chatterbox,” always talking and asking questions. Yet in front of acquaintances, I often became quiet and shy. That strange paradox in my personality later pushed me to read Surrounded by Idiots, hoping to understand people — and perhaps myself — a little better.

Even in those days, I felt a quiet pleasure in going to school. Maybe that’s where my heart truly belongs — somewhere between textbooks and desks. Or maybe I simply loved meeting friends.

One of my warmest memories from Indore comes from the summer of 2018. As I grew older, I began to appreciate the calmness of those mornings when my mother would teach me in the verandah.

At that moment, I could not have imagined how much my life was about to change.

Soon, a new chapter was about to begin.

I was going to Jodhpur — a place where I lived for only a few weeks. Yet those few weeks would end up changing me in ways I could never have expected.

And that is where the next part of my story begins…
